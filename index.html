My CV

Hi, my name is Mostafa Bamdad. I am a Ph.D. Candidate and researcher in Computational Mechanics at Bauhaus University Weimar, Germany, working under the supervision of Prof. Timon Rabczuk.

My research lies at the intersection of AI4Science, scientific machine learning, and computational mechanics. I focus on developing physics-aware and numerically reliable machine learning methods for modeling and simulating complex engineering systems.

I have experience with physics-informed neural networks, neural operators, Fourier-based methods, finite element simulation, phase-field modeling, and data-driven surrogate models for nonlinear and time-dependent systems. My broader goal is to build accurate, interpretable, and physically consistent computational tools for mechanics and engineering applications.

My representative research interests include PENCO, HAMNO, PINNs, PINOs, FNOs, F-FNOs, U-shaped neural operators, DeepONets, computational mechanics, multiphysics modeling, and AI-assisted simulation of PDE-governed systems.

Research project / manuscript in preparation

PENCO: Physics–Energy–Numerics-Consistent Operator for 3D Phase-Field Modeling

💬 Conference

Advancing Scientific Machine Learning in Industry

Workshop, TU München Institute for Advanced Study, Germany, October 15–16, 2024

I participated in this workshop and presented a poster on scientific machine learning. The event brought together academic and industrial experts to discuss recent progress in SciML, including physics-informed learning, universal differential equations, numerical analysis, and scalable methods for industrial applications.

The workshop highlighted the role of SciML in connecting data-driven learning with physical modeling and addressed practical challenges in deploying reliable machine learning methods for complex real-world engineering systems.

Machine Learning and PDEs — #MLPDES25 Workshop

FAU Research Center for Mathematics of Data, Erlangen, Bavaria, Germany, April 28–30, 2025

I participated in the #MLPDES25 workshop on Machine Learning and Partial Differential Equations at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg. The workshop focused on recent theoretical advances and applications at the interface of machine learning, PDEs, numerical simulation, and scientific computing.

Topics included machine learning for PDEs, universal approximation, data-driven classification, neural networks, and numerical simulation in mechanics, bio-mechanics, micro-fluidics, and related scientific domains.
